How to think about your employees!

Articles & Blogs • Main Category • 02/11/2017

Employees as Partners!

Employees should be thought of as people you train, nurture and develop!

When successful businesses think about their customers, they don’t just think about them as a way to make money. They think about them as long-term partners. They want their customers to keep coming back!

In the same way, employees shouldn’t just be thought of as people who just work for you. They should be thought of as those you’d like to train, nurture and develop.

In the short term, whilst it might be costly for a company to increase employee welfare, in the long run, safeguarding employee satisfaction usually, pays off! A company that cares, after all, is often reciprocated with a workforce that cares – and the result is a very virtuous circle!

Extrinsic rewards should be reasonable – you shouldn’t overpay your workers, or cut back on necessary training. But in return, you should expect your workers to give back their fair share. Many management theories about how to motivate your workers are precisely about trying to achieve just this.

Of course, employee welfare doesn’t just mean extrinsic rewards. Pay and holidays are one thing, but so is employee involvement in decision making, ensuring they get the right to exercise their vote, and their voices and ideas heard!

Welfare also means how credibly you, as a company, communicate back to your employees - about the future, the past, the good news, if tough times lie ahead - and whether your employees believe you or not.

Today, none of the above are easy. If you’re a start-up, everyone chips in - every waking hour - and everyone strives to be innovative – all the time - evangelical about the company to outsiders, at every turn and corner. These are behaviors and attributes you cannot easily incentivize through a contract!

How can you achieve this?

  • First, you try to find employees that are truly aligned with the values and goals of your organization – ideally, those that will - and want to - do good for the firm – even if not explicitly rewarded for it.
  • Second, you view your employees – as you do your customers - as long-term partners of the company - as those you train, nurture, and develop.
  • Third, you respectfully involve employees in decision making.
  • Fourth, you ensure management recognizes their needs as a person – not just a widget in the production process.
  • Fifth, you change the way you think about your employees!
  1. Think about their development - This might involve not saying that “you have to work over the weekend”, even though that might mean getting something a day late! It might mean giving that employee the chance to give that presentation when maybe you think you could do a better job!
  2. Think about your employees as a resource to learn from. So rather than thinking “this is my way of doing things”, a lot of the things they have might just be better ideas!
  3. See your employees as huge sources of competitive advantage. Don’t just think about building factories, buying machines, and getting the latest technology. Some of the most important things are intangible rather than tangible. What you want are people who “fit” and can “add” most to the organization the best! So, look to the person’s personality, character, ambitions… background!

It’s difficult to get all of this right, and satisfy everyone – but this is why companies that do end up hiring the right workers for themselves AND treating their workers well, are able to develop sustainable competitive advantage!
