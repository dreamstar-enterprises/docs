The Importance of Business Marketing?

Articles & Blogs • Main Category • 15/12/2015

You might sometimes hear company management complain about how much they’re spending on marketing. They’d rather spend their budget on something else such as: Research and Development, Product Development, Logistics, and Supply chains, or Information Technology.

All of these are important, (they are necessary just to make a product or sell a service) but then so is the Marketing!

Some might say that a product or service that has proven out to be innovative, or cutting edge, needs no marketing - it will sell by itself. Customers who like it will vote with their feet and become repeat purchasers, and advocate the product by telling their friends about it. This is true, but what if the word doesn’t spread? What if you have a great product or service, and no one ever gets to know about it?

This is a problem that many investors and often small businesses, face, and we all know of the saying “No sales, No Business”.

Marketing itself, of course, covers many activities: Advertising, Public Relations, Promotions, Media Planning, overall Strategy. These are a basket of activities htat communicate your company’s mission (purpose), vision and values. It tells others the image you want to portray of your product or service. So, really it’s best not to just overlook it!

Here are a few reasons why I think Marketing is important for a business to be successful:

Creating Awareness

For any product or service to succeed, it must be known. So creating awareness in the community in which it operates to the relevant market segments is important. People will get to know about your offering and value proposition. This gives a chance for your product to be discovered by prospective customers.

Generating Sales

As customers become more aware, marketing then turns that awareness into a growing interest and desire, that may possibly turn into to an action of purchase. Here, making it easier for customers to share their positive experiences is likely to generate more sales. More referrals can be then created, as friends and family also get to know. Eventually, a tipping point will be reached!

Reputation

Reputation is important! A strong marketing effort will help to reinforce this. You need an excellent service and exceptional product, of course, but this can be strengthened through activities such as active participation in community programs, and effective communication, both internally and externally, about your product’s or service’s most distinguishing attributes. Marketing your business this way today could create an invaluable social asset for you to sell your products tomorrow.

Competition

Competition is a nice word. Why have a monopolist who can command prices, when awareness of prices and features can help keep prices competitive - so you can more easily win over consumers? By learning more about competition and customers, you can start to align your communication efforts accordingly, such as via social media.

Loyalty

If a marketing plan builds in the right incentives (frequent flyers, prizes, coupons, vouchers), with a focus on creating customer satisfaction and retention, this can also result in very strong loyalty, that should translate into greater future profits.

Learn new Metrics (KPIs)

Peter Drucker once said “What gets measured, gets managed” – so whether you’re marketing a bakery or enterprise software company, an effective marketing campaign will teach you powerful metrics such as: Cost Per Acquisition (CPA), Total Lifetime Customer Value (LCV), and Average Revenue Per User (ARPU), and tell you a lot more about how your customers behave, so you can then optimize your plan.

Profiling your customers

Customers are out there in the market that exist for every type of business. But great marketing should make it easy for them to find you, by allowing you to build up a customer profile! An ideal profile might include things such as age, income, hobbies, interests and occupation. A well-built profile will help you better target your marketing campaigns, searching for useful patterns and characteristics. Moreover, going a step further, and digging deep into the data, will allow you to spot trends and detail that you can then hone in on, to sell to the people that matter most to your business!

Creating a strong Brand

“A brand for a company is like a reputation for a person. You’ll earn a strong reputation by trying to do hard things well” – Jeff Bezos. Smart marketers will try to create a brand that is easy to remember. A strong brand has its benefits…you don’t need to remind customers that they exist. But without any kind of Marketing, you’ll struggle to develop a powerful and memorable brand.
