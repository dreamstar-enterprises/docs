UK Film & TV

Articles & Blogs • Main Category • 07/04/2016

So what is there to talk about in UK Film & TV?

Plenty of reasons to celebrate!

The UK has created some of the most influential, and celebrated TV in the world. It has a long history in filmmaking and TV production, and is recognized globally, with critical acclaim.

Well-known films include historical dramas such as The King’s Speech, Mr. Selfridge, and Parade’s End, to imaginative fantasies such as Harry Potter and Doctor Who, to even more contemporary successes such as the Bond Movies, and Slumdog Millionaire.

Production and post-production have also been highly valued in markets such as Hollywood, with many of the biggest box office successes of recent years filmed in the UK.

Creativity and Innovation?

Some filmmakers and TV producers have been recognized around the world for their craft, originality and creativity. Take the examples of The Office, and Wallace and Gromit, and scripted dramas such as Sherlock and Downton Abbey.

The visual effects side of the industry, CGI, has also wowed audiences, in films such as Gravity, the Dark Knight Rises, and Harry Potter Series.

Unique Locations and Facilities

Art studio facilities suitable for big movie productions, are also here, and include gritty urban scenes, unique historical sites, and attention grabbing natural backdrops.

There are also some well-respected film and educational establishments such as the National Film and Television School - the first in its 58-year history to achieve the clean sweep of 3 prize categories.

(Source: The Observer)

Competitive Market

Tax reliefs for film, high-end television and animation have made production in the UK more attractive.

This has attracted productions to Britain from Hollywood and other major US and foreign productions.

We already know that there has been a long history of attracting inward investment from the companies such as Warner Bros, Disney and the other studios.

Constant Growth

Constant growth has also been demonstrated. The sector is a major contributor to the creative industries, and contributes over £4.6 billion to UK GDP, supporting over 117,000 jobs (direct and indirect).

(Source: Oxford Economics study for BFI)

Although a little dated, annual revenues for independent television were at an all time high of £3bn in 2014.

(Source: Pact 2014 Census)

…And there you go!
