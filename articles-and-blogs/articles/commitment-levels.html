Raising your Commitment levels!

Articles & Blogs • Main Category • 01/11/2017

“Commitment unlocks the doors of imagination, allows vision, and gives us the ‘right stuff’ to turn our dreams into reality” – James Womack

People often complain about not getting something they think they “deserve” – the obstacles and problems that make life “really difficult!”

The problem often lies in the fact that people never really “fully commit” to something meaningful and worthwhile – for them! They see “commitment” as nothing more than the act of being “interested” rather than “passionate” – which sadly isn’t enough.

Commitment reaches beyond “being just interested” – it means blocking out other possible alternatives – and focusing your attention and efforts on one definitive objective! It gives you that “focus of attention” that a specific outcome will happen.

It requires a lot of energy, dedication, determination, and discipline – despite the difficulties, and inconveniences you may experience along the way.

Commitment is really about being pro-active and never backing down no matter what. How much passion can you muster inside of you to overcome any doubts in the difficult moments in your life?

When you commit, you make a promise to yourself that you will do every or something much longer term. It’s of course not very effective when you make promises you cannot keep – as it conditions you in a limiting way.

When you are committed to something, you often have a clear idea of what lies ahead, on your journey; the sacrifices you must make; the resources you must acquire; and how you’re going to respond to the challenges that await!

Get clarity into the benefits!

  • What am I committing myself to?

  • What are the benefits of having this?

Get clarity into the drawbacks!

  • What time commitments might I need to make?

  • How much money might I need to put into this?

  • What resources do I need to sacrifice?

  • What will I be unable to do?

  • What are the opportunity costs?

Get clarity on the obstacles?

  • What could derail me?

  • Externally: In what circumstances, and with what people?

  • Internally: Limited beliefs? Doubtful ? Unhelpful habits?

  • Where is resistance coming from?

  • How can I work through this resistance? What must I do?

  • If I can’t successfully handle them, how could I adapt?

Get clarity about commitment levels!

  • How motivated am I to commit to this wholeheartedly?

  • How confident am I that I can follow through with my commitment?

  • On a scale of 0 to 10, how committed am I to do this?

Commit yourself to excellence!

  • To be committed means doing the very best that you can, and being excellent in everything that you do!

  • It means consistently raising your standard to propel yourself forward and break new ground!

  • Competing against your “best-self”

  • You are simply committed to doing your best regardless of the circumstances!

Commit yourself to a vision!

  • This means outlining an inspiring vision. The vision doesn’t have to change the world – BUT it has to change “your” world!

  • It has to be something that gets you up in the morning – full of passion and energy, that life around, that keeps you focused and inspired throughout the day!

  • Something so exciting you just cannot get it out of your mind when you sleep at night!

Create Empowering Daily Rituals

  • Have a small thing you do consistently every that helps you.

  • Something in the morning that keeps you inspired, something in the afternoon that keeps you productive, and an evening ritual that encourages reflection.

Create a Mantra to Stay Focused

  • There will be times when you get distracted, so you need a trigger to help you get back on track.

  • It can be in many forms – an emotional anchor you condition your nervous system into. An object (a photo, statue, or quote) that helps direct your focus and attention.

  • A mantra is an affirmation you say to yourself that you will not lose focus or get off track. For instance:

    • Let’s get back on track and get this done…

    • I am focused and ready to take the next step…

    • I am ready and committed to this outcome…

Avoid pleasures and temptations.

  • Some of the most prevalent commitment killers are those small pleasures and temptations that often sidetrack us.

  • Things that feel so nice in the short term they are impossible to resist.

  • Ask yourself:

    • What is this temptation distracting me from?

    • What am I trying to avoid doing? Why?

    • How can I make what I’m trying to avoid doing more pleasurable and fun?

    • What are the long-term consequences of indulging in this temptation?

Don’t overcommit

  • Every commitment you make isn’t always a good commitment.

  • Wrong commitments are just as bad as half-hearted ones!

  • Push yourself gradually, and know your limits and levels of ability.

  • If you don’t have the experience, skills, resources or time, think twice before taking the plunge.

  • Ask yourself:

    • Do I have the necessary skills and experience to follow through with this?

    • Do I have the adequate knowledge and resources to support my efforts?

    • Do I have the time in my schedule to give to this?

Also…

  • Remember that going forward without fully understanding what you’re getting yourself into does not work off “commitment”, but rather off of “hope”, which is in a sense an unrealistic commitment that can completely derail your efforts!

  • Lastly, do what you do joyfully, ungrudgingly, with gleeful enjoyment, and unwavering passion! Let that be your drive and differentiator!
