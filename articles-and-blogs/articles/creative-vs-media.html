Creative Agencies vs Media Agencies! - why all the confusion?

Articles & Blogs • Main Category • 12/03/2019

I see and hear lots of confusion around these two terms. There are many good answers, reflecting the fact that the business of making and producing advertising isn’t all that well-defined. But here is one that explains it well.

Creative Agencies

As a general rule, Creative agencies are focused on originating advertising and producing it.

Their activity includes but isn’t limited to: photography, videography, web development and graphic design for banner ads, websites, television ads, any sort of advertisement, graphic or video that you can think of…(the really cool stuff! - even as cool as the art of film making!)

These creative (or advertising assets) are then used across various media channels: print, broadcast, online, digital, etc. A creative agency’s forte will therefore be in generating creative ideas and creative output!

Successful creative agencies will also typically have strong planning and development capabilities, as great creative output requires getting to amazing consumer insights!

Advertising Agencies

Advertising agencies, however, generally bring a much broader set of skills to the table, including media planning and buying.

These agencies will typically work across multiple media channels, both planning and managing integrated advertising campaigns – covering things from social media, digital advertising, web site development, search engine site optimization, placing of ‘ads’ on newspapers, magazines, direct mail, billboards, TV, radio, and online, and so on.

Advertising agencies will, basically help with:

  1. How to segment the market,
  2. Which segment to target, and
  3. How to position your offering in that segment (Segmentation, Targeting, and Positioning!)

These activities will inevitably include things such as understanding customer journey, some persona development (to better understand the customer, their use case, their behaviour, etc.), some competitive analysis, developing and refining the brand proposition, lots of data and analysis to get insights from.

It’s not a hard science, but it is also not easy. Advertising involves many layers—including art and design (the creative agency part), ad placement, and frequency — and it is typically the most expensive part of all marketing plans, and increasingly, as the world of print advertising shrinks, people are finding more creative ways to advertise!

Now, most Advertising agencies make advertising — it’s just that some have more experience in knowing what to do with it and how to integrate the communications into the target consumer’s lives in a more effective way than others. But agencies are always innovating!

Advertising is not Marketing!

Advertising is only one component of the overall marketing process - that part of marketing which involves directly getting the word out about your business, product, or service straight out to those you want to reach most. Nearly all ads will have the name of the sponsor (and very often a recognizable logo), for example.

Marketing, however, involves much, much more! If you think of marketing as a pie, then the whole pie can be divided into advertising, market research, media planning, public relations, community relations, customer support, and sales strategy. Advertising, while the most in-your-face slice of the pie, is still only one slice of that pie.
