Leading a fulfilling and motivating career!

Articles & Blogs • Main Category • 21/12/2019

What makes for an awesome career?

  • Find a handful of incredible mentors who you can learn a lot from! Be attentive to what they have to say, and practice what you think will be most effective & useful.

  • Turn any abstract, and idealistic notions you may have of “pursuing your deepest passions” into something practical and implementable - something you can devote more of your time and effort to.

  • Recognise that there will always be people who are much smarter than you (more street smart, academically smart, business savvy, or emotionally intelligent) - but look forward to the one day you might be amongst them!

  • Recognise that there will be people who are more passionate about the things you hold a mere interest in – they are people who devote an incredible amount of time to their passion!

  • The key for “career awesomeness” is therefore finding and pursuing your truest and deepest passion! This then means that other people being better than you, will never take away from the enjoyment, fulfilment, and fun that you get from enjoying doing what you truly love!

  • So how to you find your passion? You try to answer 3 important questions

Where do you want to be in 5 years time?

  • E.g. a Senior Business Leader in the Engineering & Technology industry? The Finance industry? The Media & Entertainment industry? The Marketing industry? Working across what (one, two, three…) business functions?

  • E.g. Working for an already well-established global company / or medium sized enterprise / or fast growing start-up - with smart, motivated, driven people that want to find innovative solutions to challenging real-world problems?

  • Or, (if you believe you’ve seen something that you can better manage on your own) starting something completely new for a few choice and loyal customers?

Remember your career is not always about what you do, but about discovering who you are! It’s something you will be doing in the office, outside the office, in the morning, afternoon, and night!

It will never be defined by your “job title” but will be something you will be doing, even when you have gotten to the very top. It’s still going to something that is going to continue to motivate you!

So, think about what motivates you? (i) finding and giving other people honest and trustworthy advice? (ii) finding undervalued assets and making opportunistic investments into them? (iii) leading and helping others less fortunate than you? (iv) the creation and dissemination of knowledge? (v) radical innovation and creative problem solving? (vi) some combination of all of the above?

What do you enjoy, or wish you could do in your spare time?

…this will help reveal your true passions!

  • What extra-curricular activities do you pursue?

  • When you pick up a copy of The Economist, are you more interested in the economics and finance section, or the business and entrepreneurship section?

  • Do you like and enjoy the experience of selling or buying? Do you have experience in selling? Or prefer teaching? Teaching people about leadership? Inspiring others in business entrepreneurship? Passing on new knowledge and ideas to others?

  • Do you enjoy Tribalism? - working with a team, and enjoying doing things with other people?

  • You might like doing all of the above, but there will be one or two of these that you actually prefer doing much more than any of the others (as measured by the time you spend on each of these activities)… this reveals your passion!

Last question: what do you see as your values?

  • Values are what make you tick - about what you centre your life around.

  • A value is not the same as a goal. A goal might be: I want to make Managing Director, running a company in all five continents of the world. That would be something really bad as a value because what happens once you have achieved MD, and visited all five continents? Then this value will fail to continue to motivate you!

  • Examples of real values include:

    • I will always seek out opportunities to learn
    • I will always respect authority
    • I value the ability to have freedom of expression and to be creative
    • I will always keep to every commitment
    • I will always hold a “team first” mentality, valuing inclusion, diversity of thought, and collaboration.
    • I will always work in a way that demonstrates integrity, trust, respect for others, regardless of their background, level, or circumstances.

  • Remember, a value is not something you can check off your to do list! It is not a responsibility that you can discharge just by doing it once. It’s something that will continue to drive and motivate you!

  • Once you have chosen your values you then define a path that is consistent with these values.

  • The secret to a happy life is finding out what you love to do and then find someone who will pay you (handsomely / fairly) to do it! = ]

  • Thinking back about your values:

    • If you want to respect authority, then hierarchical careers will probably be better for you
    • If you prefer to have freedom of expression, and to be creative, then entrepreurship might be something of greater interest to you
    • If your value is that you want to help other people realise their potential, then you might find venture capital, or becoming a teacher as something extremely fulfilling
    • If your value is to take care of the well-being of others, then maybe becoming a doctor is something you might find extremely rewarding

So, remember…

  • Where do you want to be in 5 years’ time?

  • What do you enjoy or wish you could do in your spare time?

  • What do you see as your values?
