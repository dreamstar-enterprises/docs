Lead with Gratitude!

Articles & Blogs • Main Category • 03/11/2017

It’s no secret that mind-set can affect your mood and behaviour. Plenty of research shows that the way you “think” about yourself and the world around you can change the way you learn, create success, deal with resiliency, even how your immune system functions.

You might often think that whether you’re happy or unhappy is completely down to luck, or external circumstances. Some people are just born athletic, or born into a wealthy family, or just born charismatic. Maybe you’re stuck in a learning team with people you don’t like. Maybe things at work just aren’t going your way. Maybe you’re playing or watching your favourite sport, and somebody cheats – and you’re tempted to get angry and react in a negative way!

What’s important to emphasize here, however, is that whether you’re happy or not happy should not be purely down to external circumstances, but, as Steven Covey emphasizes – in his 1989 best-seller The Seven Habits of Highly Effective People, how you choose to respond to those circumstances. Because between stimulus and response, YOU have your great power, which is your freedom to choose how to respond to these external circumstances!

Steven Covey also coined the terms, abundance and scarcity mentality. The “scarcity mentality” is the view that if someone else wins, it means somebody else loses. There are simply not enough resources or happiness to go around. This is in direct contrast with the “abundance mentality” – the view that other people can be happy and so can you. There’s enough happiness and joy in the universe to go around!

The “abundance mentality” is useful because it trains you to not think about what other people have that you don’t, but it trains you to think about what you do have and to be extremely grateful for that!

It’s important to not forget this! Why? Because, things won’t always go your way! You might not get the job, the promotion, or the award you’ve been working extremely hard for. You might not get elected to a leadership position of a society you think you deserve. You might not get to captain or participate in a sports team which is important to you…It’s easy to get angry and frustrated in such situations!

But remember how lucky you are, and where you are! Be grateful for the things you have, and have been able to enjoy! – Things that perhaps thousands of people around the world wanted, but never had the chance to get or experience! Consider also that even if the outcome may seem negative to you – it is just possible for all parties to win in some way or another, and that every situation you may perceive as bad, has its own silver lining!

Now, it’s easy to get angry or feel dissatisfied – society has become very good as training us to be discontent. Poor customer service, late deliveries, poor quality, poor value for money. Even when you look at yourself, it’s also easy to find someone that you see as having achieved more than you – published more papers, perhaps won more medals, become a lot wealthier, received greater recognition.

It’s a dangerous mind-set to have. If you always compare yourself to others, a) you’re going to be in perpetually discontent – or you’ll always manage to find a reason to be; and b) by constantly measuring how you stack up, this can make it very difficult for you to feel genuinely happy about other people’s success – especially, perhaps, those very close to you.

So, take a moment to practice gratitude and think of all the things you have to be grateful for! For example:

  • How you get clean water when you turn on the tap!

  • How you have a roof over your head, a hot shower, or comfy bed to sleep in everyday!

  • How you have a supportive family and friends who wish you well in your life endeavours, and who are always there to care for you!

  • How you enjoy and can give back to the community you live in a way that makes you feel plentiful, creative and inspired - like a responsible citizen!

  • How the job you are in allows you to enjoy new opportunities, meet new people, and open yourself out to new experiences!

  • How you could book a ticket to almost anywhere in the world, and both afford and take the time off, to go there

  • How almost any day you could book a table at your favourite restaurant, and afford to eat there

It requires great discipline…

  1. To look at what you have, not what you don’t!
  2. To learn how to share your gifts and provide value by serving those who would benefit most
  3. To confidently share what you do - through say a personal brand presence, online or in person
  4. To look at things from a larger, more holistic perspective
  5. To consciously and pro-actively create enjoyable mastery experiences
  6. To think like a beginner, putting yourself into uncomfortable states of “not knowing”, so you are always learning, and can live an unlimited, full and satisfying life

But by practicing these things, by practicing the art gratitude, you’ll slowly begin to develop that abundance mentality - that feeling of always being happy, fulfilled, and inspired in what you do, no matter what the circumstances!!!
