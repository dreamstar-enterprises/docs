A few reasons why everyone should be ambitious!

Articles & Blogs • Main Category • 12/12/2015

Something I heard this week really caught my attention, and just really surprised me! A close colleague at work was told to not be too ambitious - and that his blue skies thinking was being unrealistic.

Now, whilst I believe that being realistic and practical are important, especially when setting goals in a business context, having an ambitious vision is incredibly important too!

Here is why:

Much of life is there for the taking, but you have to reach for it!

In business and life, treasures are typically not handed to us, but not all of them involve unbelievable amounts of work. Some things may need just a little bit of pursuit. Luck, however, favours the doers rather than the wait ‘n’ seers.

To put it another way, what are the chances that we will get what we want, if we don’t go after it?

Ambition is more important than talent

Granted, ambition alone doesn’t guarantee success, but having a lot of great talent without ambition is unlikely to get you much either.

Deliberate action will lead to skill, and what a lot of people don’t realize is what great things their talents might uncover when they leave their comfort zone.

Strong ambitions leads to attributes needed for success

Positive side effects to an ambitious feat include things such as persistence, more experience, greater discipline, heightened determination and better creativity! Not every effort will lead to results, but the more ambitious we are, the greater the chance of developing these attributes.

Ambition provides clarity

If our actions are not inspired by a great ambition, this can lead in the very troubling situation of getting muddled. With strong ambition, what is desirable yet unreachable to the unmotivated is merely a destination to the ambitious.

Ambition creates satisfaction

One of the most gratifying elements in life is being able to set a worthy and difficult goal and reach it after a long struggle.

In my view, there is no real success without ambition. Even if there was, it would be a pity and unsatisfying to stumble into success with ease. There would be no pride in having overcome a challenge, and no sense of achievement.

Trying to accomplish something ambitious is character building

Strength comes from facing difficulties and successfully enduring. This is how we develop courage and how life itself evolves. You live and you learn!

Finally, consider the alternative

Consider the words that might describe someone who lacks ambition: dull, lazy, conformist, dependent, boring, and lethargic. None of these sound remotely appealing, so which one would you rather be?
