The Power of the Many!

Articles & Blogs • Main Category • 17/04/2017

Here are a few interesting insights from a book I am currently reading by Mark Bates, called “Be the Best”. Mark has been a director and manager of a variety of Private Equity backed and Public Companies, in the U.K., and I was fortunate enough to learn several valuable lessons from him, when in our venture capital and private equity course when I did my MBA.

The book is a wonderful read - full of practical advice on building great teams and businesses, and is very readable and userable, full of layers of meaning in each subject.

A full copy can be obtained from Amazon here: Be the Best

Summary

Here is a short summary of a stand out chapter for me, on “The Power of the Many“…

Lessons of strategy – the Power of the Many….

Lesson no. 1 - An effective strategy needs to be simple to get people on board!

The importance of this lies in “The Power of the Many” – i.e. endeavouring to get more people to believe in your vision and strategy, to understand it, and make it their own, so they can generate the necessary action themselves.

As you do this, “The Power of the Many” then becomes a very powerful force, as all employees will want to drive forward in a direction set out by a unifying strategy, and as a consequence, will likely take everyone else along with them.

Very few things are as powerful in business as this!

After this, and once success slowly starts to happen, there will be a surge of confidence, as even the initial detractors will see this new strategy as a way to success.

The overall result should be that more people will then want to get on board to do more of it!

This should also lead to a team environment where everyone will want to use their brains, hands, and talent to help the team and business become the “Best it can be”!

How do you achieve such change?

Firstly, have a simple strategy in place!

Keep repeating it until it sinks in, and keep testing and challenging it others with by getting them to play it back for you.

Encourage your staff to put the strategy into action, as part of their personal objectives and departmental plans. You only need to give encouragement and support.

The important bit here is to let them work out for themselves what it means, and how to generate good ideas for implementing it.

Once they start to see its value as they put it into action, they will only want to do more of it.

After this, you should give away the power on how to implement it. So, within reason, and with the right support, allow your people to make choices that wouldn’t be your own - so you can look for the bigger gains that come from empowering them, as they get to see the vision and strategy as their own.

This will also give your staff the opportunity to get much more out of their working lives if they really get behind it, and want to deliver it!

Long term business plans are usually implemented when the “The Power of the Many” gets going, so remember to give away some of that power to your staff, to deliver a successful vision, and watch them get cracking!
