Implementing A.I./deep learning into enterprise environments

Articles & Blogs • Main Category • 29/02/2020

1. Start small

First, choose one part of your organization where you can implement A.I. and run a pilot there. If it works, you will be able to do the same in other departments. If it doesn’t, you will be able to improve it, before spending time & money on implementing A.I. in the whole organization.

2. Know what you want to achieve

A.I. and Deep Learning have become buzzwords but you don’t want to have it just as a fancy addition to your business. That’s why you need to identify the area that A.I. can improve. The A.I. use case should address a specific pain, and be it an internal one within your organization or one of your customers.

Think about the issues that A.I. is proved to solve successfully: prediction, automation, classification. You can predict, e.g. customer behaviors and preferences, demand for products, prices or resources.

3. Find out how your team feels about A.I.

Most barriers to successful A.I. adoption are human-related! People, in general, still don’t have a full understanding of A.I.. There are cultural barriers such as resistance to change, fear of the unknown – or the even stronger, feel of failure, shortage of talent, and the lack of strategic approach towards A.I. adoption.

4. Get your data ready

You can’t really think about A.I. without thinking about your data. A.I. is able to process huge amounts of data, find patterns and predict behaviors or trends, but it cannot (and will not) give you any predictions if it’s not fed with large volumes of relevant data.

It can be information collected through your service (e.g. customers’ purchase history, demographic data, on-site interactions, etc.), excel data files containing information about your recent sales, services, and orders, or any other data, including that from your CRM, ad campaigns, email lists, traffic analysis, social media or public information, e.g. about your competitors or the prices of resources.

5. Find people who have experience in A.I. implementation

Let’s face it: A.I. is not something that you or your developers will learn over the weekend. You can either try to build your own team. This can be extremely difficult as A.I. for business is an emerging field and the number of experts is limited. Or find a vendor who has experience with A.I. implementation and outsource.
