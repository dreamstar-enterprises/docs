Amazon - Love it, or Fear it!

Articles & Blogs • Main Category • 29/12/2019

The New York Times recently helped coin a term for the top-five tech companies that are increasingly dominating our lives: The Frightful Five, or better known as: Apple, Google, Microsoft, Amazon, and Facebook.

The top of his list? Amazon.

We are increasingly dependent on Amazon for buying stuff and entertaining our friends & family. Worryingly, Amazon’s reach goes deeper than that, deeper than any other company inside or outside the tech world. And the company’s grip on our lives only seems to be getting stronger.

Here are just a few examples:

  • Cloud computing. Amazon Web Services powers many of the apps and websites you use every day.
  • Artificial intelligence. Amazon has quickly become a leader in A.I. thanks to its Alexa assistant, which has opened up a new world of voice-powered computing.
  • Logistics. Through Amazon Air, Amazon plans to use drones and its own planes to deliver goods. It’s also experimenting with autonomous trucking. Many have speculated that one day Amazon won’t need to rely on UPS, FedEX, or the Postal Service to deliver stuff.
  • Entertainment. Amazon has dumped millions into original TV programming and movies. It also runs a streaming music service, and lets you buy digital music and video.
  • Food. Between Whole Foods, those futuristic grocery stores, and the Fresh delivery service, Amazon is poised to be one of the largest grocers in the country.
  • Health. According to some sources, Amazon is thinking about getting in the prescription drug business.
  • Retail and e-commerce. This one is self-explanatory.

Amazon’s influence extends further to other industries too, indirectly through CEO Jeff Bezos’ personal investments:

  • News media. Bezos owns The Washington Post and a small percentage of Business Insider.
  • Outer space. Bezos owns a rocket company, Blue Origin, that’s building reusable rockets.

That’s a lot of stuff that affects you every day from a company that started selling books online back in the 90s. Now it’s hard to find a need Amazon can’t fill.

That raises some serious, potentially scary questions. Should one conglomerate have that level of control over the future of so many vital industries people rely on? What kind of check will there be on that power, if any?

Most of the verticals Amazon is involved in are still dominated by traditional companies. But it’s clear that there’s a strong possibility we’re accelerating toward a future where there’s a digital layer on top of everything we do. And the company best equipped to deliver all is Amazon. There’s literally no one else in a position to compete!

And the real losers are likely going to be found in medium-sized and small businesses with fewer resources. In particular, the sellers and brands that rely on Amazon’s marketplace for a bulk of their sales could face even bigger hurdles, as Amazon launches more private label brands and runs overly aggressive ads that take away sales and undercuts the competition!

Some of Amazon’s business practices may warrant further regulatory scrutiny. For example, Amazon’s policy preventing third-party sellers from offering lower prices on another site raises legitimate antitrust concerns, but for the most part, the giant will probably remain off the hook, as it uses its growing power for the good of customers, by offering lower prices and more products.

Of course, people do seem to forget that Amazon have helped small businesses compete against large brands by taking care of many of the infrastructure and logistics problems, they would otherwise struggle to accomplish on their own. There are millions of small and medium-sized businesses around the world selling in Amazon’s stores.

Love it or fear it, we live in the age of hyper-convenience and incredible discounting, and there’s little doubt that Amazon has helped galvanize a business culture that puts consumers first. In the long run, Amazon will make some people nervous as its tentacles touch and disrupt entire industries. But in the end, as long as Amazon continues to deliver stellar prices to consumers and doesn’t abuse its market power, the company will be able to write the next chapter of its incredible success story.
