Mindfulness!

Articles & Blogs • Main Category • 09/04/2017

What is mindfulness and why do you need it?

Ever driven someone and realized you remember nothing about your journey? Most people have!

These are all common examples of “mindfulness” – or going on automatic pilot.

In our modern and busy lives, in our efforts t constantly multi-task, it’s very easy to lose awareness of the “present moment”, as we try to juggle home, work, finances, etc.

We are continuously distracted, dwelling on past events, or trying to anticipate the future.

As a result we are not “present” in our own lives. We fail to notice the good things about our lives, and fail to hear what our bodies (one of the most important things to look after!) are telling us, or we start to poison ourselves with toxic self-criticism.

Becoming more aware of your thoughts, feelings and sensations may sound obvious, but learning to do this in a way that suspends judgement, and self-criticism can have an incredibly positive impact on our lives!

Mindfulness is not something that will eliminate life’s pressures, but it will help us respond to them in a calmer manner that benefits our hearts, head, and body

So what are the ABCs of mindfulness?

A is for greater awareness – just being more aware of what you are thinking and doing – what’s going on in your mind and body

B is for “just being” with your experience – avoiding the tendency to go into auto-pilot, and worsening problems by creating your own story!

C is for seeing things and responding more wisely – Pausing, not rushing into, and/or creating a gap between our experience and reaction, to make wise choices!

So what do you need to do?

Practicing mindfulness involves paying attention to yourself in a particular way, on purpose, in the present moment, and non-judgmentally!

You’ll gradually learn how to slow down any brain chatter or automatic and habitual reactions, and experience the (beauty of…) present moment as it really is!

You will always experience thoughts that creep into your head uninvited. This is fine…its only natural, but it’s how you respond to them that’s really important. You can choose to get annoyed, or acknowledge the thought, let it go without judgement, and retain your focus in the present moment.

With practice, and as your neurons fire together, you’ll slowly start to respond to things more wisely, rather than on autopilot.

So, in summary, what are its benefits?

  • Slow down and stop automatic and habitual reactions (doing and thinking without taking that momentary pause!)

  • Respond more effectively to complex or difficult situations

  • See situations more clearly

  • Become more creative

  • Achieve balance and greater resilience at work and home!

  • Greater satisfaction and peace of mind!
