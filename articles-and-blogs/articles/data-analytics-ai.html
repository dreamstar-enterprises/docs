Data Analytics and A.I.

Articles & Blogs • Main Category • 01/11/2017

What is Data Analytics?

Data analytics extracts insights from large amounts of data and turns this into value (business knowledge, ideas, even deeper insights)!

Repeating these analytic exercises in short cycles allows you to further accelerate the speed of your improvements, develop new abilities, and possibly achieve significant innovation.

What is Digital Transformation?

The transformation of activities into digital - or “digital transformation” - has become essential for companies continually looking to enhance their product service proposition.

This means combining analysis with simulation models, to reflect scenarios in a digital context – and thereby dramatically accelerating the actualization of these business ideas.

This is the foundation of continuous improvement.

Can you give examples?

In Engineering, for example, expertise plays a vital role in optimising manufacturing and formulating production plans. But expertise in this knowledge can be difficult to define and apply to wider, more efficient use cases.

With continuous, now digital, analysis, it is now possible to automate and digitize this expertise so it can be used in more business areas – resulting in better quality, and shorter development cycles, for example.

In many industries, facilities and equipment are often at the very foundation. Through the analysis of operational data, effective prevention and maintenance measures can be effectively implemented by discovering early signs of failure. By preventing mishaps and malfunctions before they occur, downtimes can be minimised, and better can be made of operational facilities.

The time series analysis of a wide variety of data can decrease the damage caused by security breaches. Worldwide security incidents increased by 38% in 2015, compared to the previous year – resulting in astounding economic losses. The potential for gains here is thus also immense.

What about A.I.?

Peter Drucker, the educator and author once said “We know two things about business: it cannot be known, and it will be different from what exists now and from what we expect.”

This unpredictability is one of the many challenges businesses face today, and it is a difficulty that keeps growing.

In the past, businesses would classify situations into categories and handle unpredictable circumstances by considering contingencies in advance of each category. But fluctuations in demand and prices, as well as diversification of individual preferences, today, are happening at an increasingly rapid pace. As business activity crosses borders, businesses also face differences in national and regional cultures and customs.

Attempting to accurately classify all of these would just result in an overwhelming number of categories. And because envisioning all kinds of situations in advance is impossible, businesses need to constantly update its classifications and approaches – requiring a great deal of effort and cost.

This is where A.I. comes in!

There are several types of A.I. being used today, but even these are somewhat limited to very niche applications. There are A.I.s that generate customer recommendations to retailers (think Amazon). There are facial recognition systems that spot faces from photo data (think your Smart Phone). There are systems that answer quizzes and play board games (think Chess and Alpha Go). There are also technologies that identify people’s faces based on online images (this Facebook).

These types of A.I. need to be designed by humans based on hypotheses of data that people would enter, are then implemented as programs, and then run, and developed for a particular problem.

The disadvantage of this is that the hypotheses envisioned by people usually do not produce results that surpass human ideas!

However, now, certain types of A.I. are being developed that can learn from voluminous amounts of data and make judgments of their own, eliminating the need for people to set up the hypotheses in advance, and finding solutions that humans have not conceived. You (the human) simply set the outcome, and with a clearly defined goal, the A.I. can and learn from massive amounts of data, and present.

This is where many now say is where the real power and future of A.I. lies.

To me, the future looks exciting!
