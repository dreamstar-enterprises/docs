    Possible improvements to Payroll in Film & H.E.T.V.

    Market Research • Starlight • 07/03/2022

    Better Payroll Solution for U.K. Film & H.E.T.V.

    ×

    Overview

    I had an enjoyable time working for several months on an H.E.T.V. production for Apple Original Productions, near London. (It was nice to be working on something directed by Stephen Spielberg and Tom Hanks!)

    Though I had a nice time working in the Accounts department processing the Payroll for a very large Crew (850+) / week, I definitely felt there was scope to modernize and streamline the way work was done.

    A lot of paper gets consumed on a Production that processs large volumes of paperwork. Film and H.E.T.V. is quite unique as Payroll gets processed weekly (rather than monthly), and the make-up of the workforce is more varied compared to a tradional company (as there are many more freelances and self-employed workers that work through their own company, with a mixture of both weekly, and daily workers)

    Though Covid had accelearted the shift towards the use of paperless systems (all our Timesheets at Apple were digital), approval workflows are still not that well connected. Documents tend to get approved in very large batches (e.g. large batches of invoices saved into a single Adobe.pdf file (around 200 pages), accompanied with the relevant backup as extra pages in the same pdf). A single rejected invoice can holds up an entire batch, and payroll!)

    Filing documents from large and disparate PDFs (Payslips, Timesheets, Invoices, etc.) is very time-consuming, and even more of a problem when Timesheets don’t get signed on time, as already filed documents have to be re-opened, and updated to include the fully signed Timesheets when they do get signed.

    Embedded documents (within digital envelopes) don’t always get opened and reviewed. Late Upgrade letters, or signed Timesheets often means making retrospective changes (sometimes going back 2-3 months) for already issued Contracts, and Timesheets, with the additional paperwork that had to be prepared and filed for this.

    V.A.T. Invoices can also be hard to track, as these are often sent late, or lost through the constant stream of emails bombarded into one inbox.

    Final Payroll Jounrals are also not always very neat, and incorrect bank details is also something that needs chasing!

    So, I feel, with the above said, there is definitely room for improvement. To this end, and because I’m interested in seeing how modern Technology can used to develop better connected, more secure, flexible, and streamlined systems, I spent some time outlining what I saw and experienced as the main problems (6 main problems), and then how each of these problems could potentially be solved through better software. I have shown these below. Payroll, afterall, should be seamless!
